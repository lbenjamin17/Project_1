About My Island Antigua & Barbuda

Antigua is a beautiful island located in the northern section of the caribbean island chain. The country is pinacle of beauty boasting of 365 beaches, fun excursions and rich culture. Antigua was first discovered by Christopher Columbus is 1492 and from there the rest is history. Through years of slavery, which ended on the 1st of August, 1834, to becoming a british territory; Antigua stood the hardships boldly. On 1st Novemeber, 1981 Antigua & Barbuda gained its' independence from the british and thus paved the way for the Country it is today.

National Symbols of Antigua & Barbuda

The National Symbols are :

  • National Food: Pepperpot and Fungi

Pepperpot is a dish that comprises of cooked spinach mixed with pickled pig and chicken. Fungi (FOON-GI) is cornmeal that has water added and is churned to produce a hard cake texture.

  • National Fruit: Antigua Black Pineapple
  • National Flower: Dagger Log
  • National Tree: Whitewood
  • National Sea Animal: Hawksbill Turtle
  • National Animal: European Fallow Deer
  • National Bird: Frigate Bird (Man-o-war)
  • National Stone: Petrified Wood

Pretrified wood occurs when wood is buried for an extented period of time with volcanic ash and lava, thus it become fossilized.

 

Carnival

Carnival is a festival that celebrates freedom and emancipation for slavery.These festivities take place during the month of July and August through various activities such as:

  • T-shirt Mas
  • Jouvert (JOO-VEY)
  • Queen Show
